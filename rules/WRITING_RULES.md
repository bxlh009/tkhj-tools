# TKHJ Tools editorial rules v2.0

These rules apply to both automated drafts and manually curated pages.

## Editorial scope

TKHJ Tools has two tracks:

1. Learning: exam methods, study systems, original practice, and decision tools.
2. AI: product changes, practical workflows, prompt design, and evidence-based decision guides.

The daily target is one publishable Learning article and one publishable AI
article. Frequency never permits publishing a failed draft.

## Truth and authorship

- Attribute work to the TKHJ Tools Editorial Team.
- Never invent a teacher, student, customer, tester, credential, score, quote,
  benchmark, usage count, outcome, or personal history.
- Do not claim hands-on testing unless a real protocol and retained evidence were
  supplied to the generator.
- Treat product announcements as vendor claims, not independent findings.
- Omit a fact when supplied sources do not support it.
- Label original examples as non-official practice.

## Source discipline

- Every draft must receive at least one source URL as input.
- Time-sensitive AI claims should use a primary source when available.
- Exam formats and scoring claims must link to the official exam provider.
- Sources belong in a final `## Sources` section.
- Never invent or autocomplete a URL.
- Paraphrase source material; do not reproduce long passages.

## Learning articles

A Learning article must contain a precise learner decision, a repeatable method,
at least two original practice examples, explained answers, boundaries, and
official source notes. Do not promise score gains or use composite success stories.

## AI articles

An AI article must separate confirmed facts from interpretation, give a concrete
reader decision or workflow, state limitations, and list sources. RSS summaries
are discovery inputs, not evidence for fabricated tests, benchmarks, prices,
quotes, or broad market conclusions.

When there is no qualifying daily news event, use a configured evergreen AI topic
with official documentation. Never create a news placeholder merely to meet quota.

## Structure and style

- Use normal Markdown headings, lists, links, and tables when they improve clarity.
- Prefer specific nouns and actions over hype.
- Avoid filler FAQs, forced anecdotes, fake emotion, deliberate typos, keyword
  stuffing, repetitive introductions, and promotional CTAs.
- Use the correct domain: `tkhjtools.top`.

## Automated quality gate

Reject a generated draft before saving when it:

- is outside its requested length range;
- lacks sources or useful section structure;
- contains invented authority, outcomes, social proof, or placeholders;
- lacks original practice and reasoning for Learning;
- lacks limitations and a reader decision for AI; or
- is 30% or more similar to an existing draft in the same track.

Passing the gate means “eligible for publication automation,” not independent
confirmation that every source claim is true.
